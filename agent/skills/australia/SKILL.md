# Australia Market Context

## Market Overview

Australia has a mature tech ecosystem with a strong SaaS culture, deep design-tool heritage (Atlassian, Canva, Figma heritage), and an increasingly export-focused startup scene. The market is smaller than Southeast Asia but wealthier per capita, with companies that often expand globally from day one.

## Key Regulatory Signals

- **ASX listings**: Companies listing or preparing to list on ASX = governance tool demand, investor relations needs.
- **R&D Tax Incentive**: Australian government refunds up to 43.5% of R&D spend. Companies claiming this = R&D-active, tool-hungry.
- **ASIC (Australian Securities and Investments Commission)**: Regulatory compliance signals.
- **Export Market Development Grants (EMDG)**: Companies receiving EMDG = actively exporting, likely scaling sales teams.
- **Consumer Data Right (CDR)**: Open banking equivalent. Fintechs implementing CDR = infrastructure demand.

## Cultural Context for Engagement

- **Communication**: Casual professional. Direct but friendly. First names are standard. Avoid jargon.
- **Decision-making**: Relatively flat hierarchies. Quick to trial, slow to commit enterprise-wide. Start with team-level pilots.
- **Work culture**: Work-life balance valued. Don't expect after-hours responses. FY25 trends toward async-first.
- **Sales approach**: Lead with ROI and time-to-value. Australian buyers are skeptical of hype. Reference local customers.
- **US expansion is a rite of passage**: Many Australian SaaS companies expand to the US. Companies in this phase need sales enablement, CRM, and compliance tools.

## ICP Fit Indicators for Australia

Strong ICP signals:
- Company raised Series B+ with US or UK expansion plans
- R&D tax incentive claimant (check annual reports)
- ASX-listed or preparing for IPO
- Hiring US-based sales team while HQ remains in Australia
- SOC2 or ISO27001 certification in progress (compliance tool demand)
- Announced enterprise tier or enterprise customers
